Basic Chili

The following recipe is a modified version of You Suck at cooking's recipe. Chili is a dish that is very easy to make your own as it has endless customizable. There is no wrong way to make chili, but this is a good way to start.

Ingredients

  • 2 Pounds Ground Meat (Beef, Turkey, Chicken, etc...)
  • 1 pound Onion (Yellow or White)
  • 1 Green Bell Pepper
  • 4 Cloves of Garlic
  • 1 Tsbp Olive Oil
  • 28oz Crushed Tomatoes
  • 2 Tsbp of Chili Powder
  • 1 Tsbp of Ground Cumin
  • 1-3 Tsp Kosher Salt (Adjust to taste)
  • 1 Tsp of Black Pepper
  • 1-2 Cans of Beans (Kidney, Black, etc...)
  • 1 Cup of Water

Directions

  1. Chop Onion and Bell pepper as desired
  2. Brown and cook meat on medium heat in your largest pan, you may need to cook in seperate batched if it does not fit. Drain excess grease if needed
  3. In a large dutch oven or pot sautee chopped onion and bell pepper on medium heat for about 5 minutes or until onions are golden brown
  4. Chop garlic cloves and add to pot right when vegetables are ready, sautee for 1 minute while being careful not to burn
  5. Add ground meat and Crushed tomatoes to the pot. Stir constantly to incorperate
  6. Drain Canned beans into sink and add to pot
  7. Once ingredients are mixed well, add spices and salt. Adjusting salt to taste
  8. Add water and lower heat to low simmer for 30 to 60 minutes, depending on desired thickness
  9. Stir every 5 minutes to prevent burning
  10. Serve and enjoy
